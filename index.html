Interests

I grew up in a time where there were no role models for me as I was learning to code and entering the industry, so I try to give back as often as I can. I strive to be the person I needed when I was a kid, or at the beginning of my career, for others in my local area and beyond.

  • Presenter, Eugene Web Devs (August 2018 and August 2019) Topics include: WordPress security, working on open source projects, building a portfolio, and using Github.
  • Meeting Leader, Eugene freecodecamp Meetup (Summer 2019 to Present) I help facilitate meetings and assist anyone with questions about their coding projects.
  • Hack For A Cause Steering Committee Member (Fall 2019 through April 2020) I am the volunteer coordinator and mentor wrangler for the next annual event, helping to create a solid support staff for the hackers and the event.
  • Eugene Coding Initiative Founder (2018 to Present) I create opportunities for both minors and adults in the community to learn about technology and how they can advance their knowledge of the topic. I hold original events and programs, as well as support other groups with their own programs.
  • Grant Winner, US Ignite (Fall 2018 through Spring 2019)
  • Hack For A Cause Mentor (April 2018 and April 2019)
  • Speaker, TAO Ignite (February 2019) Topic: Normalizing diversity in technology education.
  • Presenter, Open Eugene Festival (December 2019) Topic: Open education and what it means.
  • Volunteer Coder-in-Residence Instructor, Connected Lane Coder-in-Residence Program (Spring 2018 to Present)
  • Gigabot Master, Connected Lane Coder-in-Residence Program (December 2017 to Present)
  • Eugene Women in Tech Co-Leader (2018-2019)
  • Board Member, Churchill High School Engineering Academy (September 2015 to Present)
